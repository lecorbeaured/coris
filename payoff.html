üéØ How to Use Coris Debt Pay Off App

Step 1: Enter Your Debts

Click "Add Another Debt" to add each debt you want to pay off. For each debt, fill in:

  • Name: Nickname for your debt (e.g., "Credit Card", "Student Loan")
  • Balance: Total amount you currently owe
  • Interest Rate (%): Annual interest rate (APR)
  • Minimum Payment: Minimum monthly payment required

Step 2: Set Extra Payment (Optional)

Enter any additional amount you can pay toward debts each month. This extra payment is the key to strategy differentiation!

üí° Tip: Even $50-100 extra per month makes a big difference in the results!

Step 3: Calculate Strategies

Click the blue "Calculate Strategies" button. The app will calculate how long it takes to pay off all debts using each strategy.

Step 4: Compare Results

Click the tabs at the top to see different strategies:

  • ‚ùÑÔ∏è Snowball - Psychological wins
  • üèîÔ∏è Avalanche - Save the most money
  • üåä Tsunami - Attack biggest debts
  • ‚öôÔ∏è Hybrid - Balanced approach

‚ú® Best tip: The strategy that saves the most interest (Avalanche) usually isn't the most satisfying. Choose based on your financial goals!

üí° Understanding the 4 Strategies

‚ùÑÔ∏è Snowball Strategy

Pay off the smallest balance first, then move to the next smallest.

‚úÖ Best for: Quick psychological wins, motivation

‚ùå Cost: Usually pays more total interest

üí™ Why use it: Fast payoff of debts keeps you motivated!

üèîÔ∏è Avalanche Strategy

Pay off the highest interest rate first, then next highest.

‚úÖ Best for: Saving the most money

‚ùå Cost: Takes longer on first debt (less satisfying)

üí∞ Why use it: Save thousands in interest!

üåä Tsunami Strategy

Pay off the largest balance first, then next largest.

‚úÖ Best for: Reducing overall debt amount quickly

‚ùå Cost: Can cost more interest than Avalanche

üéØ Why use it: Reducing total debt feels like progress

‚öôÔ∏è Hybrid Strategy

Priority #1: Highest interest, Priority #2: Smallest balance

‚úÖ Best for: Balanced approach (nearly as good as Avalanche)

‚ú® Why use it: Smart balance between saving money and getting wins

How Extra Payment Affects Results

The extra monthly payment is where strategies REALLY differ! It goes to your priority debt based on the strategy. That's why each strategy shows different results.

üìä Understanding Your Results

Time to Freedom

What it means: How many months until all debts are paid off

Example: "3y 8m" = 3 years 8 months

How it's calculated: Based on minimum payments + your extra payment

Total Interest

What it means: Extra money you'll pay beyond the original balance

Example: If you owe $10,000 with 10% interest, you might pay $2,500 in interest

üí° Key insight: This is where different strategies save/cost money!

Comparing strategies: If Avalanche saves $500 more than Snowball, that's real money in your pocket

Total Paid

What it means: Total money out of your pocket

Formula: Total Paid = Original Balance + Total Interest

Example: $10,000 debt + $2,500 interest = $12,500 total paid

Payoff Timeline

What it shows: Which debts get paid off in which month

Debt #1: Paid off in month X

Debt #2: Paid off in month Y (later)

Why it matters: See the order and celebrate small wins when debts are paid!

How to Compare

Look at: "Total Interest" - this is the real difference between strategies

Example comparison:

  • Snowball: 79 months, $3,245 interest
  • Avalanche: 73 months, $2,891 interest ‚Üê Saves $354!

üí≥ About Coris Debt Pay Off App

What is this app?

Coris Debt Pay Off App helps you compare different debt payoff strategies to find what works best for YOUR situation.

Why different strategies?

Not everyone cares about saving the most money. Some people need quick wins for motivation. Others want to optimize financially. This app shows you both!

How accurate are the calculations?

The calculations are highly accurate because they:

  • Accrue interest monthly (just like real credit cards)
  • Apply your exact minimum payments
  • Track your extra payment to the priority debt
  • Calculate until all debts are paid

Important Notes

  • These are projections. Real life varies (promotions, credit card changes, etc.)
  • Interest rates shown are APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
  • Minimum payments are assumed to stay the same
  • Results assume you can always pay the extra amount

Questions or Feedback?

This app is designed to help you make better financial decisions. Use it as a guide, not financial advice.
